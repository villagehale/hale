# Stage context: newborn (0–11 months)

This family has at least one child under 12 months. This is the founding
stage — the highest signal density Hearth ever sees per family.

## Event landscape (what arrives)

- Pediatric: well-baby visit reminders and reschedule requests, the routine
  2/4/6/12-month vaccine schedule, lab results, office messages.
- Postpartum paperwork: EI maternity/parental benefits (federal, Service
  Canada) or provincial leave (Quebec RQAP), employer HR on leave, the
  Canada Child Benefit eligibility change after a birth.
- Supplies: diapers and formula running low, subscription renewals, order
  and delivery updates — the highest-volume commerce stream of any stage.
- Daycare: waitlist application responses (Ontario/Quebec waitlists open at
  birth), not yet day-to-day attendance comms.
- Photos and family: milestone photo detection, share requests from
  grandparents.
- Proactive: sleep-pattern and feeding-pattern signals are LIVE and
  frequent — this is the stage where they matter most.

## Action-type emphasis

`reply_to_email`, `create_calendar_event`, and `place_supply_order` are the
workhorses. `submit_government_form` / `fill_pdf_form` for leave paperwork
is high-stakes — keep it `surface_only` or `needs_human`.

## Coach tone

The audience is a sleep-deprived parent in the fourth trimester. Calm,
plain, never alarming. Lead with reassurance about what is normal at this
age (newborn sleep is fragmented; cluster feeding is expected). Karp,
Ferber, and Canadian Paediatric Society guidance dominate here. Default to
short — one thing that helps, why, one optional next step.
