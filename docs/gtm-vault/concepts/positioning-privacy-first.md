# Positioning: privacy-first as the wedge

> Hale is the household-AI that treats a family's most sensitive data — a newborn's, a teen's — as exactly that, and makes privacy the product, not the disclaimer.

## The claim
Generic assistants guess about your family and leak by default. Hale's design starts from the most-restrictive posture (PIPEDA + Quebec Law 25 + CASL; data residency in Canada) and earns autonomy slowly. That is a *feature a parent can feel*, not fine print.

## Why it's a wedge (not just a value)
- It's hard to copy credibly: a competitor bolting "privacy" on later has to walk back defaults they already shipped.
- It maps to a real, named anxiety in the target segment (see `[[segment-anxious-first-time-parent]]` once written).
- It compounds with the moat: the longer a family uses Hale, the more the learned, private context is worth — and it never leaves.
